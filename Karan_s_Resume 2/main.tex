\cvevent{}{Practical Subgraph Learning for Anti-Money-Laundering on Blockchain Graphs \hfill \textit{\small{Jan '26 – May '26}}}{}{}
{\vspace{-0.28cm} \small \textit{\textbf{M.Tech Project | Guide: Dr.\ John Augustine, CSE Dept., IIT Madras}}\\
\small \textit{\textbf{Technologies}: Graph Neural Networks, Subgraph Representation Learning, GLASS, Neighbourhood Sampling, Blockchain Analytics}}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item Engineered a per-batch CSR-based sampler for the GLASS framework on the 49M-node Elliptic2 Bitcoin graph, cutting peak GPU memory by two orders of magnitude (\textasciitilde 0.09~GB) and enabling fixed-width architecture sweeps on a single 32~GB V100.
  \item Characterised three neighbour-sampling strategies (per-source NeighborSampler, GraphSAINT-RW, 1-hop EgoNet) on four GLASS synthetic benchmarks; established per-source sampling as the structurally safe choice for disconnected target subgraphs.
  \item Designed and ran a 12-cell, 7-seed (84 GPU runs) controlled ablation isolating four factors --- node features, depth, normalisation, fanout --- with Welch's $t$-test on every comparison; quantified per-axis effects and uncovered a multi-factor interaction.
  \item Resolved the four-fold published PR-AUC gap (0.208 vs 0.816) on Elliptic2 into one dominant feature-handling decision, two memory-economy artefacts, and one interaction-bound normalisation choice; identified two qualitatively distinct configurations reaching PR-AUC \textasciitilde 0.88 within the pipeline.
\end{itemize}
